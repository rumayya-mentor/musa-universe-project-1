09:50 – 10:40 · Assertiveness Training
Three scenarios — practise responding
50 min
The Assertiveness Spectrum:
Passive → say nothing, avoid, shrink yourself
Assertive → say it clearly, calmly, respectfully — this is the goal
Aggressive → attack, threaten, get angry

Assertive people are heard and respected. Passive people get walked over. Aggressive people push others away. You are building the middle path.
✏️
Paper Task — Role-Play Responses
For each scenario, write THREE responses: Passive, Aggressive, and Assertive. Then role-play the Assertive response with Dad.

Scenario 1: A classmate says: "You have a funny accent. Did you even go to school before?"

Scenario 2: Teacher asks the class a question. You think you know the answer but aren't 100% sure. Everyone is quiet.

Scenario 3: A group invites you to skip class to hang out. You don't want to.

For each scenario also write: What would passive cost you? What would aggressive cost you? What does assertive give you?
📋 Hand to Dad when done.
  • Write Passive, Aggressive, and Assertive responses for all 3 scenarios.20 min
  • Role-play each assertive response with Dad. Repeat each until it feels calm and natural — not angry, not apologetic.20 min
